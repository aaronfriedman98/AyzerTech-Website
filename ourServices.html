Mitigation is a crucial, initial step in the insurance claim process which helps reduce the extent of the loss to the property as is mandated by your insurance policy. The purpose of mitigation is to not only minimize further damage to the property but to also provide a necessary scope of work that gives a glimpse into the rebuild process that will take place in the near future. Examples of mitigation in a claim involves taking prompt action to prevent and protect from any additional damages and may include: securing the premises, boarding up the property, addressing water leaks and damages, clearing out the damaged property, and/or temporarily fixing damaged structures and contents amongst other actions. Sometimes, negotiations do not always go as planned. Appraisal is a process mandated by insurance law in which either the insured or the insurance company (usually authorized by the insured) can choose to opt out of standard communication and negotiation methods and resolve their disagreements in a similar fashion to arbitration. Once one party formally demands appraisal, an “umpire” or fair, third party is elected and agreed upon by both parties to decide on the eventual outcome of the claim. Once an umpire is decided, usually an inspection of the property is conducted once more with all parties involved and the umpire leads the discussions whereby both parties have to abide by certain deadlines for submission of documentation, responses etc. Ayzertech has a plethora of experience in these matters and has proven to be very successful in the past when appraisal is warranted and initiated. Ayzertech can help guide you throughout this atypical experience and can help choose an umpire and appraiser to advance your claim all the while ensuring to advocate on your behalf every step of the way.
